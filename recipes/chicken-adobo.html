Chicken Adobo

A number of specialists in the history of Asian cuisine claim that the Filipinos were preparing adobo prior to Spanish colonization. One of the earliest methods of food preservation is cooking meat in vinegar. This also applies to pork adobo, another well-liked version of the recipe.

Even though adobo has undergone various iterations throughout the years, chicken adobo is still among the most well-liked—and for good reason! For people of all ages, chicken adobo is a comfort dish because of its flavor—juicy chicken in a luscious, umami-rich sauce. We all can't get enough of this delicious lunch, kids and adults alike!

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken
  • 3 piecies dried bay leaves
  • 4 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 6 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorn

Steps

  1. Combine chicken, soy sauce, and garlic in a large bowl. Mix well. Marinate the chicken for at least 1 hour. Note: the longer the time, the better
  2. Heat a cooking pot. Pour cooking oil.
  3. When the oil is hot enough, pan-fry the marinated chicken for 2 minutes per side.
  4. Pour-in the remaining marinade, including garlic. Add water. Bring to a boil
  5. Add dried bay leaves and whole peppercorn. Simmer for 30 minutes or until the chicken gets tender
  6. Add vinegar. Stir and cook for 10 minutes.
  7. Put-in the sugar, and salt. Stir and turn the heat off.Serve hot. Share and Enjoy!
